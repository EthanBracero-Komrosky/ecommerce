Final Recommendations

1. UX & Website Optimization
Don't Touch the Checkout Flow! The conversion rates from Checkout Start to Purchase are excellent (80%+). This indicates the technical payment flow is frictionless. 
Action: Do not redesign the checkout page right now, since you risk breaking what is already working.


2. Marketing Strategy
a. Stop Over-Investing in Social for Sales! Social media is driving 30% of the traffic (Volume) but has the lowest conversion rate (Efficiency). You are likely paying for "window shoppers." 
Action: Shift the budget away from "Traffic" objectives on social ads and focus on "Retargeting" or "Lead Gen" to capture emails instead.

b. Double Down on Email Marketing! Email is the highest converting channel (~13%+ conversion rate vs ~6% for Social).
Action: Implement an aggressive email capture popup for those high-volume Social visitors. If you can get them onto the email list, the data proves that they are far more likely to buy later. You could do this buy offering discounts and promotions for those on the email list.


3. Financial & Revenue
Audit Ad Spend against AOV: We found that the average order value was ~$115. 
Action: Set a strict Customer Acquisition Cost (AOC) limit. If you are paying more than $30-$40 to acquire a customer via Social Media ads (which convert poorly), you are likely to lose money on those specific transactions.
